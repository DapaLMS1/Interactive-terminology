The Building Blocks of Medical Language

Medical terms are built from three main elements: a Prefix, a Root Word, and a Suffix. Understanding these parts is the key to quickly defining any complex medical term.

1. Prefix (The Beginning)

Indicates location (like Sub- for below), time (like Pre- for before), intensity (like Hyper- for excessive), or number.

2. Root Word (The Foundation)

Provides the fundamental meaning, typically referring to a specific body part (like Cardio- for heart), structure, or bodily system.

3. Suffix (The Ending)

Determines what the entire term is describing, usually a procedure (like -ectomy for removal), a condition (like -itis for inflammation), or a disease.
